Foundation Overview

The Schultz Family Foundation, founded in 1996 by Howard D. Schultz (former Starbucks CEO) and Sheri Schultz, creates pathways of opportunity for populations facing barriers to success. With $225 million in assets and $14.6 million in annual giving, the foundation focuses strategically on opportunity youth (ages 16-24) and veterans transitioning to civilian careers.

Mission & Focus

  • Economic Mobility & Career Navigation: AI-driven workforce development tools
  • Opportunity Youth (Ages 16-24): 4.6 million young adults out of school and work
  • Veterans & Military Transition: 250,000 annual military-to-civilian transitions
  • Entrepreneurship & Business Equity: $100 million commitment for diverse businesses
  • Educational Technology: AI-native engineering education and career advancement tools
  • National Service: State-led civilian service programs as opportunity pathways

Q1: What is your foundation's Economic Opportunity vision? What does massive success look like?

Our vision is centered on creating pathways of opportunity for populations facing barriers to success‚Äîspecifically the 4.6 million opportunity youth ages 16-24 who are out of school and work, and the 250,000 service members transitioning from military to civilian careers annually.

Massive success for us looks like this:

  • Economic Mobility at Scale: Young adults and veterans don't just find any job‚Äîthey find meaningful, economically mobile careers with clear pathways for advancement. We're not measuring just employment rates; we're tracking career growth, wage progression, and long-term economic stability.
  • Systemic Change: Career navigation becomes as accessible and effective as GPS navigation. Every young person, regardless of their background or network, can discover career pathways, understand skill requirements, and access opportunities that match their strengths and aspirations.
  • Reduced Time to Career Success: The gap between "I need a career" and "I have a career with growth potential" shrinks from years to months through AI-powered navigation tools that provide personalized guidance at scale.

We learned from our 100,000 Opportunities Initiative‚Äîwhich led to over 200,000 opportunity youth hired‚Äîthat employer connections work, but they're labor-intensive. AI allows us to maintain that personalized support while reaching millions instead of thousands. That's the transformation we're after.

Evidence: This vision is reflected in our $3M+ 2025 AI career navigation investment across four organizations, our focus on "meaningful and economically mobile careers" (not just job placement), and our integration of American Opportunity Index data to ensure job quality metrics.

Q2: How would you describe your foundation's overall approach to giving?

We're highly strategic and deeply engaged. Here's how I'd characterize our approach:

1. Multi-Year, High-Engagement Partnerships
We don't do one-year pilot funding. Our Mayo Clinic partnership is 5 years, $5M. Our AI career navigation investments are structured for scaling, not experimenting. We're in it for the long haul with our partners, providing both funding and strategic support.

2. Program-Officer Initiated (No Unsolicited Proposals)
We proactively identify organizations aligned with our strategy rather than responding to incoming proposals. This allows us to build ecosystems‚Äîlike our four-organization AI career navigation investment‚Äîwhere partners complement rather than duplicate each other's work.

3. Production-Ready, Not Early R&D
We fund solutions ready for deployment and scaling. Coach (CareerVillage), Seekr (Basta), CodePath's AI-native education‚Äîthese are mature products being scaled, not proofs of concept. We did the year-long research into career navigation barriers before investing, so when we commit, we're confident about impact potential.

4. Ecosystem Building Over Single Bets
Look at our AI strategy: CareerNet builds benchmarks for competitive algorithm development. CareerVillage's Coach provides career advice at scale. Basta's Seekr matches jobs with quality metrics. CodePath creates AI-native engineers. These investments work together, not in silos.

5. Founder-Led with Professional Execution
Howard and Sheri Schultz remain actively involved in strategic direction, bringing Howard's Starbucks experience with workforce development at national scale. But Vivek Varma as CEO and Marie Groark as Managing Director execute with deep program expertise and AI sophistication.

Evidence: $225M assets, $14.6M annual giving, program-officer initiated grant sourcing, multi-year commitments documented across portfolio, founder-led decision structure with professional management.

Q3: What programs or funds address Economic Opportunity? What are the biggest challenges? How do you measure success?

Our Core Economic Opportunity Programs:

1. AI Career Navigation Initiative ($3M+, 2025)
Four organizations creating AI-powered tools to close the opportunity gap for young adults with limited education and experience. This addresses skill acquisition (secondary education), reducing time to find jobs, and ensuring good, safe jobs with career growth potential.

2. SkillUp Coalition (formerly 100,000 Opportunities Initiative)
Our merger with SkillUp Coalition in 2024-2025 expanded our employer partnership network. We've hired 200,000+ opportunity youth to date. This is ripe for AI enhancement‚Äîimagine AI-powered matching across that entire coalition.

3. Veterans Transition Support (250K annual transitions)
We support military-to-civilian career transitions, though we haven't yet applied AI here the way we have with opportunity youth. That's a gap we're conscious of.

4. National Service Pathways
Our 2024 National Governors Association partnership supports state-led civilian service programs as pathways to careers for underrepresented communities.

Biggest Challenges We See:

  • Information Asymmetry: Opportunity youth don't know what careers exist, what skills they need, or how to access opportunities. Traditional career counseling doesn't scale.
  • Underemployment, Not Just Unemployment: First-generation college students get degrees but end up in jobs that don't utilize their skills or provide advancement. That's why we fund Basta's job quality matching.
  • Network Gaps: Young adults from underrepresented backgrounds lack the professional networks that middle-class youth take for granted. AI can partially democratize access to career guidance previously available only through personal connections.
  • Scaling Effective Models: We proved employer partnerships work (200K+ hires), but they're labor-intensive. How do we maintain quality while reaching 4.6 million opportunity youth?

How We Measure Success:

  • Economic Mobility Outcomes: Not just "did they get a job" but "are they on track for career advancement?" We track wages, career progression, and long-term stability.
  • Job Quality Metrics: That's why we integrate American Opportunity Index data‚Äîwe care about whether jobs provide privacy protections, career growth, and fair wages.
  • Scale and Reach: How many opportunity youth can we serve? How quickly can they access career guidance?
  • Time to Career Success: Reducing the gap between "seeking career" and "established in career pathway."
  • System Change: Are we making career navigation infrastructure better for everyone, not just our direct grantees' users?

Evidence: Focus areas documented across grants, American Opportunity Index integration (Basta/Seekr), 200K+ hires through 100,000 Opportunities Initiative, emphasis on "meaningful and economically mobile careers."

Q4: How would you characterize your foundation's perspective on AI?

We see AI as a transformational opportunity for social impact‚Äînot a threat to be mitigated, but a tool to be strategically deployed for economic mobility at scale.

As our CEO Vivek Varma put it: "AI's influence and technology is rapidly transforming the way each of us work and live and we believe there is a significant opportunity to leverage this technology to address the gaps in career navigation."

Here's what makes our AI perspective distinctive:

1. Technical Sophistication Without Tech Sector Baggage
We understand LLMs, AI benchmarking, and competitive algorithm development at a technical level. But we're not a tech foundation‚Äîwe're a workforce development foundation that happens to be extremely tech-savvy. That combination is rare. We can evaluate AI tools critically while staying mission-focused.

2. Strategic Positioning, Not Reactive Adoption
We didn't jump on AI because it's trendy. We spent a year researching career navigation barriers, identified where AI uniquely solves problems traditional approaches can't, then invested strategically. The growing popularity of LLMs created a timing opportunity‚Äîwe're riding that wave deliberately.

3. Ecosystem Thinking, Not Silver Bullet Mentality
AI isn't replacing human career advisors or employer partnerships‚Äîit's scaling their impact. CareerVillage's Coach combines AI with crowdsourced human wisdom. Our CareerNet investment harnesses competitive AI development to improve ALL commercial career platforms, not just one. That's systems thinking.

4. Production Deployment Focus
We're funding tools being deployed now, not research projects. Coach, Seekr, CodePath's AI integration‚Äîthese are serving users today. We're at the "Product" and "Scale" stages of AI lifecycle, not "R&D" or "Proof of Concept."

5. Competitive AI Development as Social Impact Strategy
Our CareerNet investment is particularly innovative: creating task-specific AI benchmarks that drive competitive algorithm development for career platforms. We're using competition dynamics among AI developers to accelerate innovation for social good. That's sophisticated.

What We're Not:
- We're not funding AI harm reduction or AI ethics research
- We're not spreading AI thin across all our programs
- We're not treating AI as its own vertical separate from workforce development
- We're not satisfied with pilots that never scale

Evidence: AI Readiness Score 9/10, $8M+ AI investment 2024-2025, sophisticated LLM and AI benchmarking strategy, production/scale lifecycle focus, year-long research before investments.

  • Strategic Objective: Move from labor-intensive, boutique career support (100,000 Opportunities Initiative model) to AI-powered tools that maintain personalization while reaching millions.
  • Ecosystem Objective: Improve career navigation infrastructure systemically‚Äînot just build proprietary tools, but elevate the entire field through open benchmarks and competitive development.

Budget Allocation:
$8M+ documented 2024-2025 in AI-specific grants, putting us solidly in the $1M-10M annual AI funding budget category. This is focused investment‚Äîall in workforce development and career navigation, not scattered across multiple issue areas.

Lessons Learned:

1. Ecosystem Beats Single Bet
Our four-organization AI career navigation investment is deliberately complementary. CareerNet improves the algorithms that power Coach, Seekr, and others. This creates network effects. Lesson: Build ecosystems, not isolated solutions.

2. LLM Timing Window Is Now
The growing popularity of large language models creates unprecedented opportunity for career navigation tools. But this window won't stay open forever‚Äîwe need to move from pilots to production while LLMs are capturing public attention and investment. Lesson: Strategic timing matters.

3. Job Quality > Job Quantity
Our integration of American Opportunity Index data with Basta's Seekr tool reflects a critical insight: getting opportunity youth into ANY job isn't success if it's underemployment. We need to match them to jobs with advancement potential, fair wages, and career growth. Lesson: Measure economic mobility, not just employment rates.

4. Production Readiness Is Critical
We're funding mature products (Coach, Seekr) and production deployment, not R&D. This came from our 100,000 Opportunities Initiative experience: pilots that don't scale don't create lasting impact. Lesson: Fund deployment and scaling, not endless experimentation.

5. Human + AI > Pure AI
CareerVillage's Coach combines AI with crowdsourced human career advice. This hybrid approach often works better than pure AI solutions. Lesson: AI augments human expertise; it doesn't always replace it.

6. Year-Long Research Paid Off
We spent a year researching career navigation barriers before making AI investments. That deep understanding helped us identify exactly where AI uniquely solves problems traditional approaches can't. Lesson: Strategic patience creates better investments.

Evidence: Grant details from 2024-2025 announcements, documented AI career navigation strategy, American Opportunity Index integration, year-long research into career navigation barriers, emphasis on production readiness and scaling.

Q7: What do you feel is most missing from the EO x AI investment space? What support do you need specifically?

What's Missing from the EO x AI Investment Space Generally:

1. Shared Outcome Measurement Frameworks
Everyone's measuring different things. We track economic mobility, but what indicators matter most? How do we compare effectiveness across different AI career tools? We need sector-wide agreement on what success looks like beyond just "number of users."

2. Job Quality Infrastructure
The American Opportunity Index is a start, but we need more robust, real-time data on job quality metrics‚Äînot just wages, but career advancement potential, skills development opportunities, workplace conditions. AI career tools can only optimize for what we can measure.

3. Bridges Between Employer-Side and Job-Seeker-Side AI
We have AI tools helping job seekers (Coach, Seekr). Employers are using AI for hiring. But these systems don't talk to each other. How do we create interoperability that benefits both sides while protecting job seekers from algorithmic bias?

4. Veterans-Specific AI Applications
We serve 250K annual military transitions, but AI career tools are designed for opportunity youth. Military skill translation to civilian careers is its own unique challenge. Where are the AI solutions for this population?

5. Evidence of Long-Term Economic Mobility, Not Just Job Placement
We need longitudinal studies showing that AI career navigation actually leads to better economic outcomes 3-5 years later. Right now, we're investing based on logic and early indicators, but the field needs rigorous long-term evidence.

6. Scale Economics That Work
LLM costs are a challenge. How do we maintain AI career tool sustainability as we scale from thousands to millions of users? We need shared learning about unit economics, cost optimization strategies, and paths to financial sustainability.

What Support We Need Specifically:

1. Peer Learning on AI Career Tool Effectiveness
We're investing $8M+ in AI career navigation, but we're learning in somewhat of a silo. What are other foundations funding? What's working? What's failing? We'd benefit from structured peer learning with other program officers making similar investments.

2. Co-Funding Opportunities for Scaling
Our AI career navigation grantees (CareerVillage, Basta, CodePath, Renaissance) could benefit from additional funders joining to accelerate scaling. But coordination is complex. How do we collaborate with other foundations on co-funding without creating grantee burden?

3. Technical Due Diligence Capacity
We're sophisticated about AI, but we're not AI researchers. When evaluating technical claims about LLM approaches, algorithm effectiveness, or benchmarking methodologies, we could use access to independent technical advisors who understand both AI and workforce development.

4. Policy Engagement on AI + Labor Markets
As AI transforms hiring and career development, policy frameworks matter. We're not a policy foundation, but we have on-the-ground implementation experience. How do we ensure policymakers understand what's working in AI-powered career navigation as they develop regulations?

5. Veterans AI Career Navigation Landscape Analysis
We've identified veterans as a gap in our AI strategy, but we don't want to reinvent the wheel. What AI solutions exist for military-to-civilian transition? What's missing? We'd value a landscape analysis to inform potential investments.

6. SkillUp Coalition AI Integration Strategy Support
Our SkillUp Coalition merger created a massive employer network (200K+ hires). How might AI enhance employer-employee matching across this coalition? We'd benefit from strategic thinking about AI integration into existing proven infrastructure.

Evidence: Documented gaps in veterans AI applications, SkillUp Coalition merger creating AI enhancement opportunity, emphasis on economic mobility measurement challenges, LLM cost sustainability questions, ecosystem coordination needs.
